Education & Early Career

Tyler attended Solon High School, where he played both offensive and defensive line and played in the 2018 U.S. Army All-American Game.

For his further education, Tyler chose the University of Iowa. At Iowa, the player began his football career as a defensive lineman before moving to play as the center before the 2019 season.

Tyler started all 13 games that season. He returned as the starter in 2020 and was named an All-American and a finalist for the Rimington Trophy.

In 2021, he was named the Rimington–Pace Offensive Lineman of the Year. Further, he also won the Rimington Trophy while being named a unanimous All-American.

Career

Tyler Linderbaum’s NFL career is off to an impressive start as he plays as a center for the Baltimore Ravens.

The player was drafted by the Ravens in the first round of the 2022 NFL Draft with the 25th overall pick after a trade with the Arizona Cardinals.

During his rookie season, he played in all 17 regular season games. He also played in one playoff game, amassing 1,074 snaps.

The player was recognized for his talent as he earned a place on the PFWA All-Rookie Team, celebrating the best first-year players in the league.

Tyler played a crucial role in the Ravens’ success, helping them rank first in the NFL in rushing yards per game (173.4) and second in rushing touchdowns (24) in 2022.

Tyler’s future in the NFL looks bright, and he is definitely a player to watch out for in the coming years.
