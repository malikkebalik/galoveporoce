In Jericho, Long Island, two 14-year-olds were killed and two other teenagers were hurt after a wrong-way driver crashed into their automobile. Just before 10:30 p.m., two automobiles crashed on North Broadway, also known as Route 106. Wednesday. According to authorities, the Alfa Romeo with the four teenagers inside was struck by a 34-year-old guy who was traveling on the opposite side of the road in a Dodge Ram. According to reports, he had a blood alcohol content of.18, which is more than twice the legal limit. The teenagers were returning home after a local tennis match after grabbing supper. A 16-year-old was in the left rear, the driver was a 17-year-old, and the two 14-year-olds were in the front and back of the car on the right side, where they absorbed the brunt of the collision.

The Dodge’s 34-year-old driver, Amandeep Singh, was detained and given minor medical attention, according to the authorities.
