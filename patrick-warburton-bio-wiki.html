Patrick Warburton: Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity, Nationality

The comedic actor was born on 14th November 1964 in Paterson, New Jersey, US. He is the son of John Charles Warburton Jr(father) and Barbara Jeanne Gratz(mother).

His father is an orthopedic surgeon and his mother is an actress.

Similarly, he has three sisters Mary, Lara, and Megan. His nationality is American and he belongs to mixed ethnicity(Irish, English, Alsatian, German and French).

In which school did Patrick Warburton attend?

He attended Saints Simon and Jude Catholic School. Later, he attended Servite High School and later transferred to Newport Harbor High School.

After that, he studied marine biology at Orange Coast College but dropped out to pursue modeling and acting.

Patrick Warburton: Professional Life and Career

In television, he has played David Puddy on Seinfeld, the title role on The Tick, and Jeb Denton on Less Than Perfect. Similarly, he played Jeff Bingham on Rules of Engagement and Lemony Snicket on A Series of Unfortunate Events.

Similarly, his distinct firm bass voice has landed his voice roles in animation including Kronk in The Emperor’s New Groove and its sequels and Joe Swanson on Family Guy.

He also played Brock Samson on The Venture Bros, Lok in the Tak and the Power of Juju video games and its television series, Ken in Bee Movie, Flynn in Skylanders, and Hugo Vasquez in Tales from the Borderlands.

Likewise, in advertising, he has played a control enthusiast in a series of commercials for National Car Rental. Furthermore, he is also the voice of Ranger, a forest ranger, in a series of radio spots for the national Smokey Bear campaign sponsored by the US Forest Service and the Ad Council.
