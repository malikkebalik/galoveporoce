Michelle Dockery is an English actor, stage artist, singer, and voice actor who is known for her television roles like Lady Mary Crawley in the historical drama series Downton Abbey, Letty Raines in the drama series Good Behavior, Lady Olivia (voice) in the animated series Amphibia, Alice Fletcher in the Western drama miniseries Godless, and Laurie Barber in the crime drama miniseries Defending Jacob. In addition to that, she has also been cast in numerous movies such as Downton Abbey,  Downton Abbey: A New Era, Anna Karenina, Hanna, The Sense of an Ending, The Gentlemen, The Courageous Heart of Irena Sendler, Many Beautiful Things, Self/less, Non-Stop, Hogfather, Poppy Shakespeare, Red Riding: In the Year of Our Lord 1974, Red Riding: In the Year of Our Lord 1983, The Turn of the Screw, and Tough Justice.

    Michelle Dockery Facts

  • Well before becoming one of the most accomplished English actresses, Michelle Dockery earned her living by collecting glasses in a pub.
  • Michelle is associated with various charities including Changing Faces, Oxfam, and MNDA (Motor Neurone Disease Association).
  • When she was younger and before she had joined the drama, she lost a number of roles because of her Essex accent.
  • During her early theater days, she was a member of the esteemed National Youth Theatre and had appeared in numerous productions at Royal National Theatre.
  • She has done volunteer work in a Syrian war refugee camp in Jordan while working with Oxfam charity group in an ambassadorial role.
  • In collaboration with the lifestyle brand Aspinal of London, she has created her own line of tote bags for both men and women.
  • Alongside Matthew McConaughey, Charlie Hunnam, Henry Golding, Jeremy Strong, Eddie Marsan, Colin Farrell, and Hugh Grant, she co-starred as Rosalind “Roz” Pearson in the 2019 action comedy film The Gentlemen.
  • Her stage credits include having been cast in productions of plays such as His Dark Materials, The UN Inspector, Pygmalion, Uncle Vanya, Pillars of the Community, Burnt by the Sun, Hamlet, and Network.
