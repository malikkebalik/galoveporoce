MoneySign Suede: LA rapper fatally stabbed in California prison before he was due to be released

 Posted on July 20, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  786 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

LOS ANGELES, CALIFORNIA: An aspiring LA rapper was stabbed to death in a California prison before he was due to be released, according to reports. Prison officials for the Correctional Training Facility in Soledad said that the death of MoneySign Suede, 22, — whose real name is Jaime Brugada Valdez — is being investigated as a homicide after discovering he was absent from a headcount check on Tuesday, April 25, around 9.55 pm. 

According to TMZ, the 'Back to the Bag' rapper was found unresponsive in another area of the prison. Prison personnel attempted to administer emergency aid but they were unsuccessful as he was pronounced dead around 10 pm. The incident has led officials to restrict prisoner movement while they conduct their investigation, with assistance from the Monterey County District Attorney’s Office and the Monterey County Coroner, the latter of whom will determine MoneySign's cause of death.

MoneySign was stabbed in the neck

Nicholas Rosenberg, MoneySign's attorney, told the Los Angeles Times the rapper was stabbed in the neck in the shower. After the news of his untimely death came to light, his rapper friends Fenix Flexin and Baby Stone Gorillas took to Instagram to pay emotional tributes.

MoneySign's short music career

The 22-year-old rose to prominence in recent years after releasing his debut self-titled album in March 2022. That same year, he released two more albums, Parkside Baby and Parkside Santa. The California native was best known for his songs 'BACK TO THE BAG', 'She Gon Choose', and 'Veteran', and was signed to Atlantic Records.

In an interview with the music blog site Passion Weiss, Suede spoke about his family, explaining that he would try to "see my people every day." "It’s a huge family because all my sisters already have kids," he said. "Each one has something like three, four or five kids. I got all sorts of nephews and nieces and s**t. I still see my people every day.

Why was MoneySign in prison?

Valdez was sent there after being sentenced in Riverside County last December to serve two years and eight months on two charges of being a convicted felon in possession of a gun, according to state corrections officials.
