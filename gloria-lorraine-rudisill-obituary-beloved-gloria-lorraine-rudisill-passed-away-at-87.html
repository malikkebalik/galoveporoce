Obituary: Gloria Lorraine Rudisill

Gloria Lorraine Rudisill, a beloved resident of Hagerstown, Maryland, passed away on January 1, 2024, at the age of 87. It is with heavy hearts that we share this news and extend our deepest condolences to her family and friends during this difficult time.

Life and Passing

Gloria Lorraine Rudisill lived a remarkable life filled with love, joy, and cherished memories. She was a pillar of strength and kindness, leaving a lasting impact on all who had the privilege of knowing her. Her passing leaves a void in the hearts of those who loved her, but her spirit will forever live on in their cherished memories.

As we gather to honor Gloria’s life, we invite you to join us in paying tribute to her at the visitation, which will be held on Thursday, January 4th, 2024, from 6:00 PM to 8:00 PM at the Minnich Funeral Home located at 415 E Wilson Blvd, Hagerstown, Maryland 21740. A funeral service will follow on Friday, January 5th, 2024, at eleven o’clock in the morning, at the same location. This will be a time for family, friends, and loved ones to come together, share stories, and find solace in one another’s presence.

In honor of Gloria’s memory, the family kindly requests that, instead of flowers, donations be made to Doey’s House at 11370 Caring Pathway Lane, Hagerstown, Maryland 21742, or to Hospice of Washington County at 1710 Underpass Way, Suite 300, Hagerstown, Maryland 21740. These organizations provide invaluable support and care to those in need, and your contribution will help continue their important work.

Family and Survivors

Gloria Lorraine Rudisill was a cherished wife, mother, grandmother, great-grandmother, and great-great-grandmother. She was preceded in death by her son Douglas Rudisill, her parents Howard R. Taylor and Margaret I. Taylor (Houser), and her siblings Ned, Ted, Gerald, Howard, Raymond, Robert, Charles, David, Richard Taylor, Dana Wittington, and Linda Everhart. She was the last surviving member of her immediate family.

She is survived by her loving husband, William Walter “Walt” Rudisill, who stood by her side through thick and thin. Gloria’s daughter, Cathy Marshall, and son-in-law John Marshall, were a source of immense pride and joy in her life. She also leaves behind her siblings Gary Taylor, Phyllis Zittle, Shirley Lowe, and Margaret “Peggy” Heleine, along with their respective spouses. Gloria’s granddaughters, Nicole Keener and Natalie Rudisill, brought immeasurable happiness to her life, as did her great-grandchildren, Amaya Pettner, Chase Keener, and Sierra Paugh. She was also blessed with a great-great-grandchild, Rosco Paugh, who brought her immense joy and laughter.

Gloria Lorraine Rudisill’s legacy of love, strength, and compassion will forever be cherished by her family and friends. May her soul rest in eternal peace.
