Ex-Vegas WR Henry Ruggs DUI Charges

In connection with a collision that ended in the death of 23-year-old Tina Tintor, Ruggs is allegedly facing charges of DUI, reckless driving, and gun possession while intoxicated.

Henry Ruggs III, a former wide receiver for the Las Vegas Raiders, won’t have his case transferred to a different court while facing three counts related to a fatal DUI vehicle accident in November 2021.

Justice of the Peace Harmony Letizia of Las Vegas determined on Monday that she lacked jurisdiction to reassign the matter to Justice of the Peace Joe Bonaventure’s courtroom, according to Katelyn Newberg of the Las Vegas Review-Journal.

On February 21, Newberg pointed out that Bonaventure had withdrawn from the case because he had made public comments about it while seeking reelection the year before.

According to the Police, Ruggs was intoxicated when driving his Corvette at 156 mph on November 2, 2021, on a Las Vegas street. He then struck Tintor’s Toyota Rav4, bursting the gas tank and killing Tintor and her golden retriever Max.

Ruggs had a blood alcohol level of 0.16, which is double the Las Vegas legal limit, according to the Police. On the residential street, the listed speed limit was 45 mph.
