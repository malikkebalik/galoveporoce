Who is Michael Jordan?

There are lots of known figures today, celebrities, players, artist give their everything to be rich, to be known by everyone. But only some remains the legend and popular for the generation to generation. Today we are talking about Michael Jordan, who is a former professional basketball player, whose name and credits will be known to every generation who are interested in sports.

Michael Jordan is an American former basketball player. He is also known as the principal and owner of the Charlotte Hornets of the National Basketball Association.

Michael Jordan was born on 17th February 1963, New York City, where another basketball player Kent Bazemore was born. Born to his American parents James R. Jordan Sr. and Deloris, Jordan is of Afro-American ethnicity and holds American nationality.  

His father worked as an equipment supervisor and mother served as a banker. At his small age, Jordan's family moved to Wilmington, North Carolina. 

He grew up with two elder brothers, James R. Jordan Jr., Larry Jordan, one older sister Deloris and one younger sister named Roslyn Jordan.

Regarding his studies, Michael joined Emsley A. Laney High School located in Wilmington, where he began his athletic career by playing football, baseball, and basketball. Jordan tried the varsity team during his sophomore year. However, he was only 5 feet 11 inches, he was considered short to play at that level. 

Later Michael became the star of Laney's Junior varsity team and scored 40-points. After the match, Michael was recruited by many college basketball programs which include, South Carolina, Syracuse, Duke, and Virginia. In 1981, Jordan received basketball scholarship at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and majored in cultural geography. 

From 1983 to 1984, Jordan signed out as HCCA College Player of the Year. Later he left the college to join the NBA in late 1984.

In the summer of 1984, Michael made appeared at the Olympics Games as a member of the U.S. Olympic basketball team. The team received a gold medal. 

Michael began his professional basketball career when he joined the Chicago Bulls in 1984. Jordan soon showed his skills and proved himself on the court. He assisted his team to make it to playoffs with an average stats of 28.2 points per game.

Moreover, in 1990, Chicago Bulls reached to Eastern Conference Finals and won their first-ever NBA championship defeating Los Angeles Lakers. 

In 1992, The Bulls defeated Portland Trail Blazers and won their second NBA championship. Amazingly, the following, Chicago Bulls received their third NBA championship title and dominated the basketball world.

Jordan played basketball for nineteen years and retired three times. Michael first retired in 1993, later in 1998, and finally in 2003.

Besides sports, Michael has been involved in endorsements too. He has worked with various brands like Nike, Chevrolet and Wheaties, Coca-Cola, Upper Deck, McDonald's and more to mention.

Is Michael Jordan Married or Single?

Michael has been married two times to date. Read below to seek into his personal life. 

Michael Jordan's First Spouse

Jordan tied the knot with Juanita Vanoy (ex-wife) in 1989. The couple shares a daughter Jasmine Jordan and two sons Jeffrey Michael Jordan and Marcus Jordan.

The couple began facing issues citing irreconcilable differences and filed for divorce on 4th January 2002. However, they reconciled after a short time. However, Michael and Vanoy again filed for divorce on 29th December 2006 citing the decision was made mutually. 

Juanita received $168 million as divorce annulment (worth $209 million as of 2019).

Michael Jordan's Second Spouse

In 2011, during Christmas, Jordan proposed his longterm girlfriend. She is a Cuban-American model Yvette Prieto. 

On 27th April 2013, Michael Jordan and Yvette Prieto exchanged their vows. The wedding was held at Bethesda-by-the-Sea Episcopal Church. Since marriage, the duo shares an identical twin Ysabel and Victoria Jordan (born on 11th February 2014). 

Michael Jordan's relationship

On 21st July 2006, Cook County, Illinois' judge determined that Michael did not owe $5 million in a breach of contract claim to his ex-girlfriend Karla Knafel. Previously, Jordan paid Karla $250,000 to keep their relationship secret. 

However, Karla cited Michael promised her $5 million for keeping it secret and not to file a paternity suit after Knafel found out that she was pregnant. A later DNA test confirmed that Jordan was not the father of the child.
