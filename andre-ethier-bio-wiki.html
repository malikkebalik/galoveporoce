Who is Andre Ethier?

Andre Ethier is an American professional baseball player.

He has played for the Major League Baseball club Los Angeles Dodgers as an outfielder.

Furthermore, he is also the Dodgers’ all-time leader in post-season appearances with 51.

In addition, Andre Ethier has also received a Silver Slugger Award in 2009, and a Gold Glove Award in 2011.

Andre Ethier: Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity

Andre was born on April 10, 1982, in Phoenix, Arizona, United States.

He is the son of Byron Ethier a baseball player and his mother Priscilla Ethier.

Moreover, Andre Ethier has six siblings, three brothers, and three sisters.

The grown-up in a family of baseball background, Andre had a keen interest in baseball and began to play from a very early age.

His ethnicity is Mexican- American.

In which school did  Andre Ethier attend?

Talking about his education, Andre Ethier attended Arizona State University and later moved to Chandler-Gilbert Community College.

Andre Ethier: Career

Andre began his career at Arizona State University in 2001.

During his tenure at Arizona, he recorded an ASU career batting average of .371, 113 runs, 27 doubles, 7 triples, 14 home runs, and 118 runs batted in.

Later on July 1, 2003, Andre Ethier signed with the Oakland Athletics. In 2006, he moved to Los Angeles Dodgers and made his debut on May 2.

In the next season, he played in 153 games while batting .284, with 13 home runs, and 64 RBIs. On June 26, 2009, the legendary baseball player hit three home runs in a game against the Seattle Mariners.

Furthermore, Andre Ethier signed a five-year contract extension with the Dodgers worth $85 million keeping him until 2017.

In his 2012 season, he hit .284 with 20 homers and 89 RBI in 149 games.

His next spell also went pretty well in which Andre managed to hit  .272 with 12 homers, 33 doubles, and 52 RBIs in 142 games.

However, in the 2016 season, he suffered a fractured right tibia in spring training followed by same another injury in the 2017 season.

At the end of the season, Los Angeles Dodgers denied extending his contract and made him a free agent for the first time in his career.
